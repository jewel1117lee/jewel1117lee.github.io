My Publication

Ribosomes in RNA Granules Are Stalled on mRNA Squences That Are Consensus Sites for FMRP Association

In neurons, reversible stalling of polysomes localizes to the granule fraction (the polysome pellet from sucrose gradients), enriched in FMRP and Upf1. Immunoblotting, cryo-EM, and ribosome profiling of 5-day rat brain granules show ribosomes stalled predominantly in the hybrid state. Profiling reveals longer footprint reads on FMRP-targeted, cytoskeletal, and RNA-binding mRNAs, mapping to reproducible FMRP-associated motifs. These data suggest sequence-dependent elongation arrest regulates local translation.

link

Puromycin reveals a distinct conformation of neuronal ribosomes

Puromycin normally enters the nascent peptide via the ribosome’s peptidyl transferase and then dissociates, but in neurons it remains trapped in a subset of hybrid-state ribosomes even without inhibitors. Biochemical assays and cryo-EM show these stalled ribosomes resist anisomycin block, allowing a simple puromycin-competition assay to measure stalling—over half of nascent chains in early hippocampal cultures are caught this way. This both validates puromycin for localizing stalled polysomes and reveals a neuron-specific mechanism of ribosome arrest.

link

FMRP Regulates Neuronal RNA Granules Containing Stalled Ribosomes, Not Where Ribosomes Stall

FMRP-rich neuronal RNA granules underpin local protein synthesis by housing stalled polysomes. In P5 FMRP-null mice, ribosome structure and stalling sites remain unchanged, but granules contain fewer FMRP-targeted mRNAs and are reduced in number; the remaining granules resist mGluR-LTD reactivation, highlighting FMRP’s role in granule maintenance rather than stall initiation.
